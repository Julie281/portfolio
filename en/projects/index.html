PocordDiscourse analysis
Applied AIpost-meetingadoption

Overview: Pocord is a product exploration focused on discourse analysis with AI in order to make post-meeting management more efficient.

Challenge: The challenge was not only to make conversation easier, but to build an experience where each person can identify what matters most to them inside a discourse or conversation. The underlying question was how to combine clear rules, useful signals and lightweight automation to support more consistent participation.

Role: I worked on product conceptualization, problem framing, participation logic and the overall structure of the experience.

Approach: The project relies on lightweight automation and design decisions aimed at reducing friction. The goal is not to add complexity, but to make the experience more intuitive, legible and easier to sustain over time.

Learning: Pocord reinforces a core belief in my work: even small or experimental products do not achieve adoption by accident. They need structure, clarity and an experience that helps people understand how to take part and why it matters.

Gamify Your LifeGamification
motivationexperiencecontinuity

Overview: Gamify Your Life explores how gamification can become a tool for structure, continuity and real motivation instead of remaining a superficial layer of stimuli.

Challenge: The challenge was to turn intention into consistency. It was not about adding mechanics for the sake of it, but about designing a system that is simple, understandable and sustainable enough to support progress without creating fatigue or artificiality.

Role: I worked on concept exploration, experience design, progression logic and the overall product organization.

Approach: The project relies on signals of progress, feedback, a sense of forward motion and an interaction model designed to sustain use over time. The aim is to balance motivation and control, without sacrificing clarity or user autonomy.

Learning: Gamify Your Life connects with a question that runs through much of my work: how to generate real adoption and maintain perceived value beyond first use. That same logic also appears in conversational assistants, digital product and AI-powered experiences.

How I structure a project

How I structure a project

My way of structuring a project starts from a simple idea: before thinking about features, you need to understand what problem deserves to be solved and what experience can create real value. That is why I approach every project as a combination of context, decisions and learning.

Problem

I identify the need, tension or opportunity that justifies the project.

Experience

I design the interaction logic, journey and information needed to make the proposal clear.

Prioritization

I separate what is essential from what is secondary so I can validate before adding complexity.

Learning

I turn every iteration into a source of signals about adoption, friction and possible evolution.
