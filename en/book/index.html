Conversaciones Inteligentes - the practical guide to designing conversational assistants that deliver real value

This book explains how conversational assistants have evolved from a technological curiosity into strategic channels of interaction between companies, customers and employees. With a clear, applied and business-oriented approach, it offers the keys to designing, implementing and optimizing conversational experiences that truly work.

What the book covers

  • How conversational assistants have evolved: from rigid flows to experiences powered by generative AI.
  • How to choose use cases, design effective journeys and define a conversational personality aligned with the brand.
  • How to measure real impact with KPIs that connect experience, operational efficiency and business results.
We are living in a moment where conversations are becoming the natural space of interaction between people and brands. In that context, conversational assistants have stopped being a technological curiosity and become strategic channels capable of transforming customer experience, team efficiency and the digital relationship of an organization.

Conversational assistants are no longer science fiction. Today they are a central part of how companies answer questions, support decisions, automate processes and integrate conversation into everyday channels such as websites and WhatsApp.

More than a technical overview, the book works as a playbook: it helps readers choose use cases, design conversational journeys, define personality and tone, integrate multimedia resources and measure impact with concrete KPIs such as adoption, efficiency, engagement, conversion and operational savings.

The core thesis is clear: a conversational assistant is not simply a system that answers. It can become a central piece of an organization’s digital transformation when it balances automation, human intervention, brand identity and continuous improvement.

Who it is for

This book is written for people who need to make decisions, design experiences or lead conversational projects with sound judgment: product managers, customer experience professionals, marketers, innovation teams, operations and technology roles.

It does not need to be read linearly. Each chapter can be consulted depending on the stage of the project, the sector or the reader’s role, so the book also works as a reference guide and practical tool.
